# Journey

Rivers Reckoning currently has a seven-map playable arc. It is mechanically complete enough to boot, choose a starter, catch creatures, progress through four region-master gates, reach the final route, defeat the green dragon, see credits, save, and resume. It is not yet a polished v1 journey.

## Beat 1: Riverside Home

Rivers begins at home in a safe village. The starter mentor introduces the investigation and offers three companions:

- Ashcat
- Mireling
- Bramble Imp

The player receives capture pods and the first companion. This beat teaches talking, starter choice, clues, and the first safe area without random encounters.

## Beat 2: Greenwood Road

The first route introduces tall grass, wild encounters, capture, item drops, and the first rival. The forest should read as a transition from safety into uncertainty: clear road, readable encounter grass, blockers at the edges, and NPC staging that does not hide tap targets.

Current quest emphasis: help with capture practice and prove Rivers can handle the road.

## Beat 3: Highridge Pass

The first region-master challenge lives at the top of the pass. The map teaches elevation, harder blockers, and set-piece combat. Winning grants Highridge proof and opens the next region.

## Beat 4: Lakehaven

Lakehaven is a village/shop beat. It should feel calmer than the pass: water, plaza, shop, NPC errands, and a stronger sense that the world has communities Rivers can help.

Current quest emphasis: delivery and trust-building around the lake.

## Beat 5: Frostvale

Frostvale introduces snow/ice terrain, cold-region creatures, and the next region-master challenge. It needs strong visual separation from Lakehaven while preserving touch-readable paths and NPC staging.

Current quest emphasis: cold-weather supply help and catching regional creatures.

## Beat 6: Dreadpeak Cavern

Dreadpeak is the hardest pre-finale region-master challenge. It should feel dangerous but not cruel: torch landmarks, cave walls, readable encounter spaces, and a clear climb toward the master.

Current quest emphasis: torch-path navigation and proof that Rivers can face the final approach.

## Beat 7: Rivergate Approach

The final route leads to the green dragon. It should combine river, shoreline, final-route tension, rare encounters, and enough quiet space to make the dragon staging readable.

The green dragon is both the finale set-piece and a rare final-route catch target. Defeating it completes the current game state and unlocks credits.

## V1 Expansion Needs

- Stronger opening mystery and clearer reason Rivers is investigating.
- More NPC arcs that return across regions.
- Quest chains that connect regions instead of isolated errands.
- Better loot pacing and optional side paths.
- More deliberate encounter-zone readability.
- More map density, landmarks, and biome transitions.
- Post-clear loop that gives a reason to continue catching and revisiting.

## Acceptance

A completed v1 journey needs a beginning-to-end user path that is fun without docs:

1. The player understands where to go next.
2. Every region has a clear identity, local problem, reward, and memorable staging.
3. Required fights and catches are fair at expected party strength.
4. Optional catches and side quests feel rewarding rather than mandatory.
5. The green-dragon ending resolves the main mystery and leaves a small post-clear loop.
